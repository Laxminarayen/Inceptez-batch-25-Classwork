9. Calculating Total Error

Total Error is the Sum of Weights of the incorrect predictions.

Stump 1 (Chest Pain) got Patient C and Patient E wrong.

Patient Weight Result

Total Error = 0.2 + 0.2 = 0.4

10. Amount of Say (Trust Score)

How much do we trust a Stump with 0.4 Error?

$$ \text{Say} = \frac{1}{2} \ln(\frac{1 - \text{Error}}{\text{Error}}) $$

Let's Plug it in:

$$ \text{Say} = 0.5 \times \ln(\frac{1 - 0.4}{0.4}) $$

$$ \text{Say} = 0.5 \times \ln(1.5) \approx \mathbf{0.2} $$

This stump gets a vote of 0.2 (Low, but positive).

12. Increasing Weight (The Penalty)

We must increase the weight of Patient C & E (The Mistakes).

$$ \text{New Weight} = \text{Old Weight} \times e^{\text{Say}} $$

Calculation:

Old Weight = 0.2

Say = 0.2

$$ 0.2 \times e^{0.2} \approx 0.2 \times 1.22 = \mathbf{0.244} $$

Their weight grew from 0.20 to 0.24.

12. Decreasing Weight (The Reward)

We decrease the weight of Patient A, B, D (The Correct ones).

$$ \text{New Weight} = \text{Old Weight} \times e^{-\text{Say}} $$

Calculation:

Old Weight = 0.2

$$ 0.2 \times e^{-0.2} \approx 0.2 \times 0.81 = \mathbf{0.16} $$

Their weight shrank from 0.20 to 0.16.

17. The Penalty Block

Why does this work?

If Stump 2 ignores Patient C, it will get 2 questions wrong (since C is there twice).

This will create a huge Error rate.

To minimize error, Stump 2 naturally focuses on the duplicates.
